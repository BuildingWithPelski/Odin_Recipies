Steak With Sauce



Description

A perfectly seared, tender steak, cooked to your desired doneness, is elegantly topped or served alongside a luxurious, house-made sauce. The rich, savory flavor of the meat pairs harmoniously with the complementary notes of the sauce, creating a balanced and indulgent dining experience

Ingredients

  1. For the Steak:
    • Steak: Boneless ribeye, sirloin, or New York strip.

    • Seasoning:Kosher salt, freshly ground black pepper.

    • Fat: Vegetable oil or butter for searing.

Directions:

  1. Cook the Steak:
    • Prep: Let steaks come to room temp, pat dry, and season well with salt & pepper.

    • Sear: Heat oil in a heavy skillet (cast-iron is great) over high heat until smoking. Sear steaks 2-4 mins per side for a nice crust (adjust for doneness).

    • Rest:Transfer steaks to a plate, loosely tent with foil, and let rest 5-10 mins (this keeps them juicy).

  2. Make the Pan Sauce:
    • Aromatics: In the same pan (drain excess fat, but leave browned bits), lower heat and saut√© minced shallots and garlic in butter for a couple of minutes.

    • Deglaze:Pour in liquid like red wine, beef broth, or brandy, scraping up all the flavorful fond from the bottom. Simmer until reduced by about half.

    • Finish:Turn off the heat and whisk in heavy cream, Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, and a knob of cold butter until smooth and emulsified. Add peppercorns if desired.
